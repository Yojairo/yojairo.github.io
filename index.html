Hello, I'm Yojairo.

Raised in Stockton, California by two Mexican immigrants, hard work and resilience were instilled in me from the start. Being born with Spina Bifida, doctors weren't sure if I'd ever be able to walk or have an average intelligence. The following was said about me when I was two years old.

February 28th, 2003: “Reception and expressive language disorder, borderline intellectual functioning, and spina bifida.”

Looking back at this statement, the adversity I faced has only motivated me to excel in everything that I pursue. Even if it was difficult to learn how to walk or speak properly, I'm proud of my history and hope to use it to inspire others. 

Today, I study Computer Science & Business Administration at the University of Southern California. On campus, I'm involved in various organizations, ranging from LavaLab, USC's premier student-run incubator, to Novus, an interdisciplinary problem-solving think tank.

Looking forward, I hope to make a positive impact at my company and community. Eventually, I hope to teach computer science to the next generation to increase representation of people from underrepresented and low-income backgrounds in tech.

As a software engineer, I have experience in various languages, tools, and frameworks. 

Languages: C++, Java, HTML, CSS, JavaScript.

Frameworks/Tools: Bootstrap, Angular, Git.

I truly believe an interdisciplinary education is the foundation of innovation. With that in mind, I chose my major and take courses that expand my knowledge across various fields.

Computer Science Courses: Data Structures and Object-Oriented Design, Discrete Methods in Computer Science, Principles of Software Development, Introduction to Algorithms and Theory of Computing.

Business Courses: Organizational Behavior and Leadership, Microeconomics for Business, Foundations of Accounting, Marketing Fundamentals, Macroeconomics for Business.

Other Courses: Immigrant America, Passing and Performance in American Identity, African-American Popular Culture, History of California.

During my first internship at Google in 2019, I was in Chicago working on a Security & Privacy team. I was tasked with developing a UX/UI feature, using TypeScript, for cryptographic keys in Google Cloud, revamping how keys are displayed. 

Throughout my second internship, I was working remotely with a Google Ads team based in Mountain View. During this summer, I developed open-source application that searches for locally-owned restaurants and determines wait times for customers. 

For the upcoming summer of 2021, I will be at Google working as a software engineering intern.

At the University of Southern California, I worked at the Viterbi School of Engineering as a Data Researcher and a Web Content Manager. Here, I was responsible for designing, developing, managing and maintaining the primary and auxiliary websites of the university.

In the fall of 2020, I worked with Dr. Jill Johnston's Research Lab to develop their website to exemplify the work and publications done by the team.
